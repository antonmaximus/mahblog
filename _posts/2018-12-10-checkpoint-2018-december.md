Failed again. It  seems I always zone out from Spring through Summer and then at the end of year, I start to track my lifts again. Next Goal: Track myself in February. Am not sure how many times I'd be tracking myself next year, but my goal is to stay close to what I feel matters to me: lifting. 

Nonetheless, it has been a very interesting year: I got a major left wrist injury while doing Cleans in early March and that extended through August, I tried to study for MCAT, and quit my stressful hospital job. Of course, his affected all of my lifts especially Deadlifts, but I should have been **aware** on what matters.

Some of the positive things that happened this year: figuring out a new meal schedule that can help me prepare for MCAT and improve daily life, discovering remineralization of teeth via coconut oil, and learning to toner. 

As a measure to K.I.S.S., I've decided to streamline on how I track my lifts: I will only be doing 1 rep maxes and will combine the videos as 1 checkpoint. I've noticed that I had separate tracking for my Squats and Deadlifts in the past. 

Furthermore, after looking at my spreadsheet data, I have some past data on my 1 rep maxes. The good news: I can use them as my starting information for all of my 1 rep maxes. The bad news: the lifts suggest they've gone down over the 2 year span. Oh well, now I am more aware.

Next Goal: Checkpoint in February 2019.
